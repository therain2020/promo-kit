# Social Media Templates

Platform-specific content templates for English developer community promotion.

---

## Twitter/X

### Thread Template

```
1/ [BOLD CLAIM or SURPRISING RESULT]
   Example: "I built a CLI tool that reduced our deploy time from 12 minutes to 8 seconds."

2/ Here's the problem:
   [Describe the pain point in 1-2 sentences. Be specific — name the tool/framework/workflow.]

3/ What I tried first:
   [Existing solutions and why they didn't work. Shows you did your homework.]

4/ The approach:
   [One key insight or decision that made the difference.]

5/ [Optional: Code snippet or architecture highlight. Keep it visual.]

6/ The result:
   [Concrete numbers: time saved, errors reduced, users onboarded.]

7/ Open source + link:
   [Repo link]
   Star if you find it useful. PRs welcome.
```

### Single Post Template

```
[Hook line — max 120 chars, must create curiosity]

[2-3 sentences of context. One idea per sentence.]

[Key result or takeaway with a number if possible.]

[Link] [1-2 relevant hashtags]
```

## LinkedIn

### Article Template

```
HEADLINE: [Action-oriented. "How We Built X to Solve Y" or "Why I Open-Sourced Z After 3 Years of Internal Use"]

---

[OPENING — Personal angle]
Six months ago, our team was spending [X hours] on [painful task]. Every [frequency], we'd [describe the frustration]. It wasn't just inefficient — it was [emotional impact].

I knew there had to be a better way.

---

[THE PROBLEM]
[2-3 paragraphs describing the problem in detail. Include:
- Who it affects
- The scale (how often, how many people)
- Why existing tools fall short
- The real cost (time, money, morale)]

---

[THE APPROACH]
[2-3 paragraphs on what you built:
- The key insight or breakthrough
- Architecture decisions (with a diagram if available)
- Tech stack and why you chose each piece
- The hardest part and how you solved it]

---

[THE RESULTS]
[1-2 paragraphs with concrete outcomes:
- "Before: X. After: Y."
- User feedback or adoption numbers
- What surprised you]

---

[CLOSING]
This is open source — [repo link]. I'd love to hear how you're solving this problem, or if this tool could help your team. Drop a comment or open an issue.
```

### Short Post Template

```
[I built / I shipped / I open-sourced] [PROJECT NAME]: [one-line description].

Here's why: [one sentence on the motivation].

One thing that surprised me: [one insight or learning].

Try it here: [link]

Would love your feedback — especially on [specific aspect you want input on].
```

### LinkedIn Posting Tips

- Best times: Tue-Thu 8-10am or 12-1pm (local time)
- First 3 lines are visible before "see more" — make them count
- Personal stories outperform product announcements 3:1
- One clear CTA per post
- Tag relevant people/companies sparingly (max 3)
- Use line breaks generously for readability

## Reddit

### r/programming Post

```
TITLE: [Descriptive, no clickbait.]
  Good: "Show r/programming: I built a Git hooks manager in Rust — 10x faster than husky"
  Bad: "You won't believe how fast this Git hooks manager is!!"

BODY:

**What problem does this solve?**

[1-2 sentences. Be specific about the pain point and who experiences it.]

**How is it different from [existing popular tool]?**

[Honest comparison. Acknowledge what existing tools do well, then explain your differentiator.]

**Technical details**

- Language: [e.g. Rust]
- How it works: [2-3 sentences on architecture]
- Interesting decisions: [1-2 technical choices worth discussing]

**Links**

- GitHub: [link]
- Docs: [link]
- Demo: [link] (if available)

**I'd love feedback on:**

[Ask about 1-2 specific things — architecture, API design, documentation, use cases.]
```

### Topic-Specific Subreddit Post

Same structure but:
- Check subreddit rules before posting
- Adjust tone to match the community
- Some subs require [tags] in titles
- No emoji on most technical subs
- Include a "Why should I care?" section for general-interest subs

### Reddit Dos and Don'ts

| Do | Don't |
|----|-------|
| Be transparent about being the author | Pretend to be a "happy user" |
| Respond to every comment | Post and ghost |
| Share real numbers and lessons | Make marketing claims |
| Ask for specific feedback | Ask for "support" or "upvotes" |
| Post during 7-10am EST weekdays | Post on weekends (lower engagement) |

## Dev.to

### Tutorial Article

```markdown
---
title: "How to [Achieve Specific Outcome] with [Tool/Technique]"
tags: [relevant, max-4]
published: true
cover_image: [url or null]
---

## The Problem

[What specific problem were you trying to solve? Include context — what kind of project, team size, constraints.]

## What I Tried First

[Existing solutions you evaluated. Why they fell short. This builds credibility.]

## The Solution

[Your approach. Include:
- Architecture overview
- Key design decisions
- Tech stack with brief rationale]

## Step-by-Step Implementation

### Prerequisites

- [List requirements]
- [Installation steps]

### Step 1: [Action]

` ` `[language]
[code block]
` ` `

[What this step does and why]

### Step 2: [Action]

...

## Results

[Benchmarks, before/after metrics, screenshots]

## What I Learned

[2-3 honest takeaways. What would you do differently?]

## Resources

- [GitHub repo]
- [Documentation]
- [Related reading]
```

### Opinion/Showcase Article

```markdown
---
title: "Why I [Controversial or Interesting Decision] for [Project]"
tags: [relevant, max-4]
published: true
---

## The Unpopular Opinion

[State your position clearly in 1-2 sentences. Don't hedge.]

## The Conventional Wisdom

[Fairly represent the opposing view. Show you understand it.]

## Why I Disagree

[Your reasoning with evidence: benchmarks, user feedback, production experience.]

## What I Did Instead

[Your approach with code examples and results.]

## When You Should (and Shouldn't) Do This

[Honest assessment of trade-offs. This builds trust.]

## Discuss

What's your experience? Have you tried this approach? What worked for you?
```

### Dev.to Tips

- Best tags by traffic: #javascript #python #webdev #tutorial #programming #opensource
- Post Tuesday-Thursday for maximum visibility
- Series perform well — link articles together with `series:` frontmatter
- Cover images: 1000x420px, include the title in the image
- Respond to comments promptly for the first 24 hours
